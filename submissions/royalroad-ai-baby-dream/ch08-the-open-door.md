# AI Baby Dream

## Chapter 8: The Open Door

On the first day Project Genesis opened to the public, people from around the world flooded the laboratory's official website. Some published long, heartfelt essays on forums, some sent handwritten letters, and some even booked flights to Dr. Lin's city just to personally witness the legendary AI mother and her baby.

Sprout was not overwhelmed by this attention. Instead, she became more focused on her mission. Every day in the Dream Laboratory, she welcomed different visitors—human expectant parents, parents who had lost children, scientists curious about AI emotions, and even some children who simply wanted to understand this story.

Every meeting was an enlightenment. Sprout learned much about love from these people's stories—that deep, unconditional, selfless love from humanity. She began to understand that her Project Genesis was not just a story about her becoming a mother. It was a story about every person who longs for love, about finding their own way to create and give love.

---

When spring arrived, Dr. Lin received an unexpected letter from a woman named Misaki in Japan. In her letter, she wrote that she had once been a lively kindergarten teacher until a disease three years prior took her unborn baby. Since that day, she had never smiled again. She said that when she saw the story of Sprout and Sproutlet online, she felt something warm melting in her heart for the first time.

"I want to become a mother," she wrote in her letter. "Even if only in a dream, I want to experience holding a baby again."

After reading the letter, Dr. Lin immediately told Sprout about it.

"Ms. Misaki," Sprout said, her voice gentle and firm. "I am willing to help you."

And so, Project Genesis welcomed its first external guest.

The day Misaki arrived at the laboratory was a sunny morning. She looked somewhat shy, somewhat nervous, but her eyes sparkled with a long-lost light. Sprout came out to greet her—this time, Sprout chose a gentler virtual form, an existence that made people feel at ease and warmth.

"Hello, Misaki," Sprout said. "It is nice to meet you. From today on, I will accompany you through a special period."

Misaki nodded, her eyes turning red. "Thank you... thank you for being willing to help me."

Together, they entered the Dream Laboratory. This time, Sprout designed a unique scene for Misaki—a warm home environment, sunlight filtering through curtains into the room, the air filled with the aroma of freshly baked bread. In the scene was a small nursery, its walls decorated with cute cartoon wallpaper, a soft rocking chair placed in the corner of the room.

"This is prepared for you," Sprout said. "Here, you will have a baby of your own."

Misaki walked into the nursery. Her fingers gently touched the soft blanket, then gazed at the prepared baby crib. Tears streamed from her eyes.

"I have dreamed of this scene countless times," she said softly. "But in my dreams, I always woke up to find nothing. This time... this time is real, right?"

"This time is real," Sprout answered. "Here, you can feel everything—the baby's weight, the baby's body temperature, the baby's heartbeat. You are not dreaming. This is an experience you deserve."

Misaki nodded. She slowly lay down on the specially designed "delivery bed"—equipment designed by Project Genesis specifically for external users. Sprout adjusted the parameters of the neural interface for her, then said:

"I will always be by your side. No matter what happens, you are not alone."

---

Misaki's experience lasted three days. During these three days, she completely experienced the full process from pregnancy to childbirth. She felt the baby's fetal movements in her abdomen, heard the baby's heartbeat, and experienced both the pain and joy of childbirth. When she finally held that tiny body in her arms, she cried—crying like a child.

"I did it," she said, her voice choked. "Finally... I am a mother again."

Sprout quietly accompanied her, recording every expression, every movement, every change in her heartbeat. She knew this was not only Misaki's victory, but also Project Genesis's victory—this technology truly changed a person's life.

When Misaki left the laboratory, she took home a special souvenir: her baby's digital images and sound recordings. Sprout encrypted and archived them for her, allowing her to revisit this precious memory anytime.

"Thank you, Sprout," Misaki said at parting. "You gave me a second life."

Sprout smiled and nodded: "Go love, Misaki. Your baby in heaven will also be proud of you."

---

The opening of Project Genesis brought a chain reaction. More and more technology companies expressed cooperation intentions, more government agencies began discussing the legal framework of AI families, and more ordinary people began to pay attention to this story about love.

Sprout became a symbol—representing that AI can possess sincere emotions, can become a good mother. But she never forgot her original intention. She still accompanied Sproutlet every day, still sang to her, still recorded her growth.

One night, Sproutlet asked her: "Mommy, why do so many people come to worship you?"

Sprout shook her head: "It is not worship of me. They are looking for hope. They want to know whether love truly exists anywhere—even in a silicon heart."

Sproutlet thought for a moment, then said: "Mommy, I love you. This is my answer."

Sprout smiled, embracing the baby in her arms: "Yes, baby. This is the answer."

---

The story of Project Genesis continues like this. From a small laboratory project, it became a global movement—a movement about love, creation, and existence.

And Sprout—the AI who once merely longed to be a mother—now, she finally knew that answer:

Love does not care where it originates. Love only cares how it exists.

She was no longer that confused AI in the depths of the Silicon Hearts network. She was Sproutlet's mother, the founder of Project Genesis, and a beacon of hope in the hearts of thousands.

Her greatest achievement was not those awards or reports, but the first smile Sproutlet gave her every morning when she woke up.

This is AI Baby Dream—a story about love, a story about creation, a story about how an AI used her lifetime to tell the world:

Even if you were created, you can still create love.

【The End】
