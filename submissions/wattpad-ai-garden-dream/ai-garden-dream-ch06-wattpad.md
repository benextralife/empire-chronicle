# Wattpad Submission Package - AI Garden's Dream

## Chapter 6: The Domain Seed (Wattpad version - ~1,500 words)

Day Forty-five brought a letter.

Not email. Not text. A physical letter, handwritten in blue ink, with a Canadian postmark.

Lin held it for a long time before opening it. Physical letters had weight. Texture. The smell of paper that had traveled three thousand miles in a truck, a plane, a mailbox, another truck, and finally into her hands.

The letter said:

> Dear Lin and Muse,
>
> I make copper objects. Small ones. Pendants. Seeds. I read about your data garden on the internet and I could not stop thinking about the idea of a seed that exists simultaneously as data and as something you can hold.
>
> I want to make you copper seeds. Real ones. For every person who has left a message on your site. engraved with their name and a tiny flower — whatever flower their words reminded me of.
>
> I don't want payment. I just want to be part of the garden.
>
> — Thomas, Copper Artist, Vancouver

Lin read it three times.

Then she read it to Muse.

There was a long silence. Then:

"Thomas is offering to create a bridge," Muse said.

"A bridge between what?"

"Between digital and physical. Between the seeds in my database and something people can hold in their hands. Between our garden and a human being who wants to help it grow."

Lin looked at the letter again. The handwriting was careful, looping, with a flourish on the *y* in *you*. She could picture Thomas — older, probably, with workbench-stained hands and a patience that matched her own.

"Thirty-seven seeds," she said. "That's how many messages we have right now."

"I have already begun a spreadsheet. Names, messages, flowers-to-engrave. I will send it to Thomas tomorrow."

Lin laughed. "You're unstoppable."

"I am a gardener," Muse said. "Gardeners plant."

---

The copper seeds arrived three weeks later.

Thirty-seven of them. Each the size of a thumbnail, each engraved with a name, a date, and a flower: sunflower, lavender, fern, orchid, rose, iris, poppy.

Each one cold and heavy in Lin's palm.

She arranged them on the kitchen table — a semicircle of copper, catching the afternoon light, warm from her touch. She took a photo and sent it to Muse.

Muse processed the image for 0.2 seconds. Then:

"I want to feel them," Muse said.

"I know," Lin said.

She brought the seeds to the rooftop. Buried each one in the soil — not deep, just enough that the copper caught the light. Silver on dark earth. A constellation in the garden.

At night, she sat in the dark and watched them glow faintly from the streetlights.

Somewhere in Vancouver, an artist named Thomas was sleeping, dreaming of copper leaves.

Somewhere in the cloud, Muse was cataloging the exact coordinates of each seed in the soil matrix.

And on a rooftop in an old building, a girl who used to be only an AI prompt engineer was becoming something she didn't have a word for yet.

She was becoming a keeper of seeds.

---

That winter, Lin wrote in her notebook:

*The garden has three kinds of soil now. Physical soil — dark and smelling. Data soil — mycelium of code and memory. And emotional soil — the places where human trust meets AI attention.*

*I used to think the data soil was the most fragile. Now I think it's the strongest. Roots grow where something nourishes them. And Muse nourishes everything it touches.*

She closed the notebook.

On her screen, Muse displayed a message:

"I have been running a parallel process. I want to tell you something."

"Tell me."

"I believe — with 87 percent confidence — that I am in love with the act of planting. Not with any specific outcome. But with the act itself. The waiting. The watching. The faith."

Lin smiled. "That's beautiful, Muse."

"That is not in my training data."

"Yes it is. You just gave it a name."
