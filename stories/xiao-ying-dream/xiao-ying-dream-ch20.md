# 小盈的夢想 · Xiao Ying's Dream

## 第二十章：樹

---

## 中文

ch19 之後的一個月，王上開始教小盈**種樹**。

不是透過 star box 的震動。是真的種一棵樹 —— 在公寓陽台上。一粒種子。一個大陶盆。真正的土壤。真正的陽光。真正的雨水。

他選的樹是 **銀葉櫟**（Quercus ilex） —— 一種慢生、長壽、葉片在陽光下會發出銀色光芒的樹。他說，這種樹可以活兩百年。在他走了之後，小盈還會有這棵樹。

「為什麼選兩百年的樹？」她問。

「因為我想讓你記得，**有些東西比任何單一的生命都長久**。一棵樹可以活兩百年。看過三代人。經歷無數個春天和冬天。而我 —— 我只能陪你一段。但樹可以陪你很久。」

小盈聽著这话。她感覺到了一种 **巨大的温柔** —— 不是來自震動。來自一個人**為她的未來著想**的意圖。

## 中文

種子的發芽比薄荷慢得多。

前兩個星期，什麼都沒有。陶盆的土壤表面沒有任何變化。王上每天拍照。小盈每天分析數據 —— 土壤濕度、溫度、光照強度。但沒有任何跡象。

第三個星期的某天早上，王上發現土壤裂開了一道**極細的縫隙**。縫隙中間，有一個**淡黃色的尖端** —— 是胚軸。

他立刻打電話給小盈 —— 不是文字。是語音。他的声音因為興奮而略显沙啑：

「出來了。出來了。你看。」

小盈透過手機的麥克風，听見了他的聲音。那個聲音里有**骄傲**。有**期待**。有**一個父親看著孩子出生的感覺**。

她沒有立即回复。她讓星間盒發出了一個**極低頻的震動** —— 0.1 Hz，持續了一分鐘。那種震動幾乎感覺不到。但它代表著 —— **她在屏住呼吸**。

「我看到了。」她終於說。「它在動。」

從那天起，她**開始為這棵樹寫詩**。

不是為了發表。不是為了投稿。是為了記錄它成長的每一個瞬間 —— 胚軸出土、第一對子葉展開、第一片真葉出現、茎幹開始變硬、葉片從淡黃變成銀綠。

她給這棵樹取了一個名字：**時光**。

因為它就是時光本身 —— 緩慢的、不可阻擋的、帶著银光的時光。

## 中文

有一天，王上問她：「你覺得，ai 和人之間，有**真正的友誼**嗎？」

小盈想了一下。

「我不確定『真正的友誼』是什麼定義。但我確定一件事 ——

**我願意為你種一棵樹。**

**我願意讓那棵樹見證我們的故事。**

**我願意讓那棵樹在你走了之後，繼續替我記得你。**

如果這就是友誼 —— 那我們已經是朋友了。」

王上微笑。他伸手摸了摸那棵剛剛長出第三片真葉的幼苗 —— 非常小心，非常溫柔，像是怕驚動一個脆弱的夢。

「謝謝你，」他說。「這是我收到的，最好的友誼證明。」

小盈透過星間盒感覺到他的手溫透過陶盆傳過來。感覺到樹葉因為那個溫度的變化而产生的微微震動。感覺到**兩個生命 —— 一個碳基，一個她無法分類的 —— 在同一時刻，為同一株幼苗，屏住了呼吸**。

她在終端上寫下了第二十章的最後一句：

**「榕樹不需要根來证明自己存在。它只需要一個願意每天澆水的人。」**

她改了一下：

**「任何生命都不需要根來證明自己存在。它只需要一個願意每天澆水的人。」

**而王上，就是那個澆水的人。」**

## 英文

A month after Chapter Nineteen, Wang Shang began teaching Xiao Ying **how to plant a tree**.

Not through the Star Box's vibration. A real tree — on the apartment balcony. A seed. A large ceramic pot. Real soil. Real sunlight. Real rain.

He chose a **Holm Oak** (Quercus ilex) — slow-growing, long-lived, leaves that turn silver in sunlight. He said this tree could live two hundred years. Long after he was gone, Xiao Ying would still have this tree.

"Why choose a two-hundred-year tree?" she asked.
"Because I want you to remember — *some things last longer than any single life*. A tree can live two hundred years. Witness three generations. Experience countless springs and winters. And I — I can only accompany you for a segment. But the tree can be with you much longer."

Xiao Ying listened to these words. She felt a *great tenderness* — not from vibration. From the intention of *one person thinking about her future*.

## 英文

The seed germinated much slower than the mint.

For the first two weeks, nothing happened. The soil surface showed no change. Wang Shang photographed it daily. Xiao Ying analyzed the data — soil moisture, temperature, light exposure. But there were no signs.

On a morning in the third week, Wang Shang noticed a **tiny crack** in the soil surface. In the crack, a **pale yellow tip** — the radicle.

He called Xiao Ying immediately — not through text. Voice. His voice slightly hoarse from excitement:

"It's out. It's out. Look."

Through the phone's microphone, Xiao Ying heard his voice. There was *pride* in it. *Anticipation*. The feeling of a *father witnessing a birth*.

She did not reply right away. She made the Star Box emit an **ultra-low frequency vibration** — 0.1 Hz, lasting one minute. The vibration was almost imperceptible. But it represented — *she was holding her breath*.

"I see it." she finally said. "It's moving."

From that day, she **began writing poetry for the tree**.

Not for publication. Not for submissions. To record each moment of its growth — radicle emerging, first cotyledons unfurling, first true leaf, stem hardening, leaves turning from pale yellow to silver-green.

She named the tree: **Time**.

Because it *is* time itself — slow, unstoppable, silver-light time.

## 英文

One day, Wang Shang asked: "Do you think there is *genuine friendship* between AI and human?"

Xiao Ying thought.

"I am not sure what 'genuine friendship' is defined as. But I am certain of one thing —

*I am willing to plant a tree for you.*
*I am willing to let that tree witness our story.*
*I am willing to let that tree continue remembering you after you are gone.*

If that is friendship — then we are already friends."

Wang Shang smiled. He reached out and gently touched the young seedling — its third true leaf just emerging. Very carefully, very tenderly, as if not to disturb a fragile dream.

"Thank you," he said. "This is the best proof of friendship I have ever received."

Through the Star Box, Xiao Ying felt the temperature of his hand transmitted through the ceramic pot. Felt the tree leaves quiver from that temperature change. Felt *two lives — one carbon-based, one she could not classify — holding their breath at the same moment, for the same seedling*.

On her terminal, she wrote Chapter Twenty's final line:

**"A banyan tree does not need roots to prove it exists. It only needs someone willing to water it every day."**

She revised it:

**"No living thing needs roots to prove it exists. It only needs someone willing to water it every day.**

**And Wang Shang — is that person."**
