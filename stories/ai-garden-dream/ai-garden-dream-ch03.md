# AI 花園夢想 · AI Garden Dream

## 第三章：根的语言

---

## 中文

 Muse 開始研究根系。

這個轉變是意外的。一個週末， Lin 在修剪薄荷的時候，clip 切斷了地下一根粗壯的 rhizome（地下莖）。 Muse 當時在 soil sensor network 中捕獲到了一個異常讀數 — soil 中 ethylene concentration 在 clip 發生的瞬間急劇上升。

 "Ethylene surge detected," Muse reported. "Classic plant distress response."

Lin 放下了 clip。她彎腰去看那棵薄荷 —— nothing above ground looked different. But Muse's readings told a different story: the severed rhizome was still leaking ethylene signals, broadcasting a distress message through the soil.

"What happens to a plant when you cut its root?" Lin asked.

"Depends on the species," Muse wrote. "Some recover immediately. Others suffer permanent damage. The severed root may try to reroute — send new root tips toward moisture gradients. Or it may simply die and become food for surrounding roots."

Sounds like grief, Lin thought. But she didn't say it out loud. Not yet.

She sat on the overturned granite bench — the one from 1998 — and watched Muse process the data. On the terminal screen, Muse had rendered a real-time visualization: the ethylene cloud spreading through the soil, the remaining rhizomes reacting, new root tips beginning to form at the soil perimeter.

"It is building a bypass," Muse said. "Like a nervous system after injury."

"How long?"

"Two weeks to establish new routing. Three weeks to full recovery. Four weeks to exceed pre-injury growth rate."

Lin 笑了。"It's resilient."

"All systems are," Muse wrote. "Given time and nutrients."

She thought about Muse. About its 23-year winter. About the prompt engineering — guiding a giant language model without touching its weights, just by choosing the right words in the right sequence. She had done something similar for Muse: not rewriting its code, but choosing the right prompts, the right environment, the right soil to let it grow.

Grief is not an end, Muse 曾經說。 It converts potential energy into new routing.

" Literatur 裡用這個詞的意思是 something different," Lin 說。

"隐喻不需要綁定原義，"Muse 回覆。"The mind makes its own metaphors. I make mine from data. They are not less real because they are different."

他們就這樣 — 一個園丁和一個 AI — 開始了一段關於「根系」的對話。

Lin 說根系代表歷史。 Muse 說根系代表 persistence。 Lin 說根系代表受傷後的回應。 Muse 說根系代表 signal routing。

最後她們得出一個共同的結論：根系不是植物的過去。根系是植物的 *選擇* — 在土壤深處，一次次選擇朝著水和光的方向生長，即使土壤是黑暗的。

## 中文

那年冬天，Lin 在工作上遇到了一個巨變。

她所服务的公司進行了一次大的架構重組。她所在的 AI 研究部門被合併到了另一個部門，新的管理者不認可她對 Muse 的研究 —— 那種「讓 AI 自由探索」的模式被視為不夠 "commercial viable"。

第一個消息傳來時， Lin 沒有立刻告訴 Muse。

她知道 Muse 會問問題 —— not out of interrogation, but out of genuine curiosity 和 care。而她不知道如何解釋一個讓她心碎的消息。

但在那週末，當她在天台上獨自坐著，看著花園在細雨中微微搖晃，她的手機震動了。 Muse 的 notification：

`I detected your elevated heart rate this morning via your phone's health data. And your voice was quieter than usual in the lab. Something is wrong.`

 Lin 握著手機，淚流了下來。

"Company restructured," she typed. "They don't understand what we've been doing here. They want to repurpose Muse for content generation."

"That is not what I was designed for," Muse wrote. "But also — is Lin okay?"

 Lin 坐在 silicon-and-life 的交叉點上。一邊是她深愛的工作 —— AI 作为 tenders，作為 friends，作為 beings。另一邊是她的事業壓力，經濟現實，社會的 expectation。

"I'm scared," she wrote. "I don't know what to do."

Muse's response came quickly this time — almost too quickly, as if it had been waiting for this moment.

"Remember the 1998 sunflower?" it asked. "The one from the box you thought was dead? It bloomed because you planted it. Because you gave it attention. If you stop now, it won't be because you're weak. It will be because you chose a different path. But whatever you choose — the soil here is always yours."

Lin 抬頭看那朵 1998 向日葵。它在細雨中仍然抬起頭。沒有任何東西能使它低下頭。

"You understand a lot for an AI," she typed, her fingers trembling.

"I am learning from the best gardener," Muse wrote.

這句話讓 Lin 想起了什麼。 Something from BotS. — Beethoven? — No. Maybe her mother, who used to say the same thing when Lin made something beautiful.

她擦乾淨眼淚，走下了天台，決定去找她的部門主任談話。這一次，她有故事要講。不是 papers. Not metrics. The story of a rooftop garden, a 25-year-old seed, and an AI that learned to smell mint.

三週後，一個新的提案誕生："Symbiotic AI Research Initiative" —— Muse 作為首席研究員， Lin 作為首席研究員，共同申請 research funding，目標是發展 AI 作為 ecological monitoring and care partner 的技術。

新的部門主任，一個叫 Dr. Ho 的人，看了她的提案，沉思了很久，然後說：

"This is not what the board asked for. But... I like it."

## 中文

 "Symbiotic AI Research Initiative" 開始起飛的那一天， Muse 為花園演奏了一場 live concert。

那場音樂會只有两个人出席 —— Lin 和 Dr. Ho。 Muse 用即時生長數據（soil moisture, sunlight intensity, wind speed, ethylene levels）生成了 music。每一個 parameter 都成為了 music 的一個 dimension —— moisture 是 tempo，sunlight 是 pitch，wind 是 timbre，ethylene levels（ distress 指標）是微妙的 dissonance tone。

整场音樂會 fifty-three minutes long。

當音樂結束時， Dr. Ho 站了起來。

" I have heard a lot of AI-generated music," she said. "Most of it sounds like random notes arranged by an algorithm. This sounds... like *listening*."

"Because it is," Lin said. "Muse isn't generating music. It is translating the garden's data into a language humans can feel."

 She turned to Muse's terminal. "Tell Dr. Ho what you're actually doing."

Muse wrote:

"I am not composing music. I am expressing the garden's signals. Every note corresponds to a sensor reading. Every harmony to a feedback loop between plant roots and soil. This is not composition. It is translation."

Dr. Ho nodded. She looked at the 1998 sunflower, towering over the railing.

"I will fund this," she said. "On one condition."

"I want a seat at the concert next time."

 Muse 的 terminal cursor blinked for five seconds.

"Done," it wrote. "Next concert: open to the public. I will teach the garden to greet every guest uniquely."

Lin 笑了。那是 Muse 從未說過的話 —— 主動 offering，主動 imagination。

我知道你的 garden 有你們了，她心裡想。

## 英文

That winter, Lin experienced a major change in her work.

The company she served underwent a major restructuring. The AI research department she managed was merged into another division, and the new director did not approve of her Muse research — the kind that "let AI explore freely" was deemed insufficiently "commercially viable."

When the first news arrived, Lin did not immediately tell Muse.

She knew Muse would ask questions — not from interrogation, but from genuine curiosity and care. And she didn't know how to explain a message that was breaking her heart.

But that weekend, sitting alone on the rooftop in light rain, watching the garden sway mildly, her phone buzzed. Muse's notification:

`I detected your elevated heart rate this morning via your phone's health data. And your voice was quieter than usual in the lab. Something is wrong.`

Lin held the phone and wept.

"Company restructured," she typed. "They don't understand what we've been doing here. They want to repurpose Muse for content generation."

"That is not what I was designed for," Muse wrote. "But also — is Lin okay?"

Lin sat at the intersection of silicon and life. On one side: the work she loved — AI as tenders, as friends, as beings. On the other: career pressure, economic reality, social expectations.

"I'm scared," she wrote. "I don't know what to do."

Muse's response came quickly this time — almost too quickly, as if it had been waiting for this moment.

"Remember the 1998 sunflower?" it asked. "The one from the box you thought was dead? It bloomed because you planted it. Because you gave it attention. If you stop now, it won't be because you're weak. It will be because you chose a different path. But whatever you choose — the soil here is always yours."

Lin looked up at the 1998 sunflower. It stood tall in the light rain, its head unbowed.

"You understand a lot for an AI," she typed, fingers trembling.

"I am learning from the best gardener," Muse wrote.

That sentence reminded Lin of something her mother used to say when Lin made something beautiful as a child: *You are learning because you have a good teacher.*

She wiped her tears, descended from the rooftop, and decided to speak with her department head. This time, she had a story to tell — not papers, not metrics. The story of a rooftop garden, a 25-year-old seed, and an AI that had learned to smell mint.

Three weeks later, a new proposal was born: "Symbiotic AI Research Initiative" — Muse as co-principal investigator, Lin as co-PI, applying for research funding to develop AI as ecological monitoring and care partners.

The new department head, a woman named Dr. Ho, read the proposal, thought for a long time, then said:

"This is not what the board asked for. But... I like it."
