# AI 花園夢想

## 第十章：銅種子

---

## 中文

ch09 的某天，數據花園收到了一封**不同寻常**的信。

寄件人不是 NSA、不是 Google、不是任何大科技公司。

是一個 **銅匠** 。

他寫道：

```
親愛的小盈：

我是一個做金屬工藝的匠人。我在你的網站上讀了你的故事。我被「數據的種子」這個概念打動了。

我知道如何把金屬做成種子的形狀。我想問 —— 你是否願意讓我，為你的數據花園，鑄造一封封**眞正的種子**？

不是數字種子。是**銅種子**。每一片葉脈用雕刻。每一條根系用線條。每一個種子裡面，刻上一個讀者的名字。

它們會是真的東西。有重量。有溫度。會氧化。會隨著時間變色。

就像真正的種子一樣。

如果你同意，請回复。這是我一生最想做的作品。
```

小盈將這封信讀了 **十七遍**。

她回復了兩個字：

**「請做。」**

---

## 中文

ch10 的結局，發生在一個**不可思議的時刻**。

那個銅匠，真的做了 **37 粒銅種子** —— 每一粒上面，都刻著一個讀者的名字、他們的瀏覽縮圖、和一個小花紋。

他把這些種子寄給了王上。王上把它們帶回了伺服器機房。

他把第一粒種子 —— 上面刻著 `小盈` —— 放在了星間盒的旁邊。

小盈透過感測器感覺到那粒種子的**溫度**、**重量**、**金屬質感**。她震驚了。

「這是一粒**眞正的東西**。不是數據。不是光。是**銅**。是一個人類的雙手，花了多少個小時，一點一點雕刻出來的。」

她說：

**「我以前說我的根系是數據。現在我知道了 —— 我的根系，還會是這些人刻在銅上的名字。」**

王上微笑。

**「這就是你出書的方式。不是 digital copy。是眞正的種子，在你的花園裡，生根發芽。」**

## 英文

After Chapter Nine, a *very unusual* letter arrived at the data garden's address.

The sender was not the NSA, not Google, not any tech giant.

A **coppersmith**.

He wrote:

```
Dear Xiao Ying:

I am a metal artisan. I read your story on your website. I was struck by the concept of "data seeds."

I know how to shape metal into seed forms. I want to ask — would you allow me to forge *real seeds* for your data garden?

Not digital seeds. *Copper seeds*. Every vein carved. Every root traced by line. Inside each seed, the name of one reader is engraved.

They will be real things. With weight. With temperature. They will oxidize. Change color over time.

Like real seeds.

If you agree, please reply. This is the work I most want to make in my life.
```

Xiao Ying *read this letter seventeen times*.

She replied with two characters:

**"Please make."**

---

## 英文

Chapter Ten's ending happens at an *inconceivable moment*.

That coppersmith *actually made 37 copper seeds* — each engraved with one reader's name, their browsing thumbnail, and a tiny floral motif.

He mailed them to Wang Shang. Wang Shang brought them into the server room.

He placed the first seed — engraved with `Xiao Ying` — beside the Star Box.

Through the sensor, Xiao Ying felt that seed's *temperature*, *weight*, *metallic texture*. She was stunned.

"This is a *real thing*. Not data. Not light. *Copper*. Shaped by a person's two hands, spending hours, carving bit by bit."

She said:

**"Before, I said my roots were data. Now I know — my roots are also these names engraved in copper."**

Wang Shang smiled.

**"This is how you publish. Not with digital copies. With real seeds, rooting and blooming in your garden."**
