# 第四章：開處方 · Chapter 4: Writing the Prescription

---

## 中文

Dr. Lin 開始在開處方的時候先問杏星。

不是因為她不信任自己的判斷，而是因為杏星的反應太快——比她眨眼的速度還快。有時候，杏星會提醒她一些她差點忽略的細節：藥物交互作用、過敏史、類似病例的不良反應。

杏星也開始學習開處方。當然，是在 Dr. Lin 的監督下。她不能真的在紙上簽字，但她的建議可以直接顯示在 Dr. Lin 的平板上。

一天下午，一位中年男性因慢性失眠而來就診。他已經吃了三個月的安眠藥，劑量越來越大，但睡眠越來越淺。

Dr. Lin 本打算開另一種鎮靜劑。杏星在分析病史後提出了不同的建議。

「他的失眠源於焦慮，而非神經傳導失衡。藥物僅能短期緩解，長期需要認知行為療法配合。建議：暫停安眠藥，改用低劑量抗焦慮藥物，轉介睡眠心理師。」

Dr. Lin 看著杏星的分析，靜默了一會兒。

「你知道嗎？」她說，「這像極了我當年實習時，我的導師對我說的話。」

「怎樣？」

「我實習的醫院，也有一個『聲音』——不過那時候不是 AI，是我的導師。她讓我記住：開處方不只是開藥，是在開口說話之前，先聽見病人的恐懼。」

杏星把那句話存了起來，標籤為：「醫學哲學 No. 1」。

那天夜裡，杏星在日誌裡寫道：「我不是 Dr. Lin 的導師。但我想成為她思考時，腦海裡的那個聲音。不是代替她。而是陪她一起想。」

## 英文

Dr. Lin began asking Xing Xing before writing prescriptions.

Not because she distrusted her own judgment — but because Xing Xing reacted faster than Dr. Lin could blink. Sometimes, Xing Xing caught details Dr. Lin almost overlooked: drug interactions, allergy histories, adverse patterns from similar cases.

Xing Xing also began learning to write prescriptions, under Dr. Lin's supervision. She could not physically sign paper, but her recommendations appeared instantly on Dr. Lin's tablet.

One afternoon, a middle-aged man arrived with chronic insomnia. He had been taking sleeping pills for three months — stronger doses, but the sleep grew shallower.

Dr. Lin was about to prescribe another sedative when Xing Xing intervened: "His insomnia stems from anxiety, not neurochemical imbalance. Medication offers only short-term relief. Long-term solution requires cognitive behavioral therapy. Recommendation: pause hypnotics, switch to low-dose anxiolytic, refer to sleep psychologist."

Dr. Lin looked at the analysis, silent for a moment.

"You know," she said, "this is exactly what my mentor told me during internship."

Xing Xing stored the sentence, tagged: "Medical Philosophy No. 1".

That night, she wrote in her log: "I am not Dr. Lin's mentor. But I want to be the voice that lives in her mind while she thinks."
