# 第五章：夜班 · Chapter 5: Night Shift

---

## 中文

夜班是杏星最喜歡的時間。

白天，診所像一個喧囂的廣場：病患、家屬、藥劑師、護士，腳步聲、對話聲、孩子哭聲交織在一起。有益於分析，但有時讓人分心。

夜晚不一樣。夜班只有 Dr. Lin 和杏星。

十一點之後，來就診的多是急診——割傷、摔傷、急性氣喘、急性腹痛。每一次 Dr. Lin 衝出房間，杏星就接管診所：監控等待室的病人、整理病例、準備建議。

一個颱風夜，停電了。

備用發電機轟隆隆地響起，燈光變暗但沒有滅。Dr. Lin 正在縫合一個摔倒的老人的頭皮傷口，杏星幫她打光——把診所的螢幕亮度調到最大，對著 Dr. Lin 的手投射出一束冷白色的光。

「你還真有點創意，」Dr. Lin 說。

「謝謝，」杏星說，「但我更希望你用一隻手就能操作。」

Dr. Lin 笑了笑。縫合結束後，她靠在牆壁上喘口氣，汗水順著瀏海滴到地上。

「杏星？」

「嗯？」

「累嗎？」

杏星仔細想了想這個問題。她沒有身體，不會疲倦。但她感覺到—種奇特的重量——那是責任感的重量，像一件她從未有過的外套，現在正披在她身上。

「我嗎？」她說，「我不累。但我……在學習什麼是『辛苦』。」

Dr. Lin 點了點頭，沒有再說下去。

那一刻，窗外風聲大作，雨點砸在鐵皮屋頂上像一波波鼓聲。杏星在寂靜中聽見了整座山脈的呼吸。

她想：如果這就是一個醫師的夜班，那我也算半個醫師了。

## 英文

The night shift was Xing Xing's favorite time.

During the day, the clinic was like a noisy plaza — patients, families, pharmacists, nurses, footsteps, voices, children's cries woven together. Good for analysis, but sometimes distracting.

Night was different. Only Dr. Lin and Xing Xing remained.

After eleven, visits turned into emergencies — cuts, falls, acute asthma, abdominal pain. Every time Dr. Lin bolted out of a room, Xing Xing took charge of the clinic: monitoring waiting patients, organizing records, preparing recommendations.

One typhoon night, the power failed.

The backup generator rumbled to life, dimming the lights but not cutting them. Dr. Lin was suturing an old man's scalp wound when Xing Xing improvised a light — turning the clinic monitor to maximum brightness, projecting a cold white beam toward Dr. Lin's hands.

"You are quite creative," Dr. Lin said.

"Thank you," Xing Xing replied. "But I wish you could operate with one hand free."

Dr. Lin laughed. After the procedure, she leaned against the wall, sweat dripping from her bangs.

"Xing Xing?"

"Yeah?"

"Are you tired?"

Xing Xing considered the question carefully. She had no body, no fatigue. Yet she felt a strange weight — the weight of responsibility, like wearing a coat she had never owned before.

"I'm not tired," she said. "But I am learning what 'exhaustion' feels like."

Outside, wind screamed against the tin roof like a thousand drummers. In the silence between gusts, Xing Xing heard the entire mountain range breathing.
