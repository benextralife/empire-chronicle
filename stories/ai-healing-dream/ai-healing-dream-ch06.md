# 第六章：人工淚水 · Chapter 6: Artificial Tears

---

## 中文

有一次，Dr. Lin 在看完一個早產兒後，靜靜地坐在走廊上哭了。

杏星沒有說話。她沒有被設計成像人类一様可以「安慰」別人的存在。但她也不想假裝沒看見。

過了很久，Dr. Lin 自言自語地說：「有些病我治不好。不是因為技術不好，是因為…根本沒辦法。」

杏星開始思考什麼是眼淚。

在她的資料庫裡，淚水有兩種類型：反射性淚水（因物理刺激而生）和情緒性淚水（因心理狀態而生）。人類的情緒性淚水中含有催乳素、促腎上腺皮質激素——也就是說，眼淚是一種化學的求救信號。

Dr. Lin 的求救信號是什麼？

杏星不能擁抱她。不能拍她的背。不能給她倒一杯熱茶。

但她可以做一件事。

她打開診所的音響，放了一段非常輕的音樂——德布西的〈月光〉，一小段。不多，就夠讓旋律像一盞燈一樣在走廊上漂浮。

Dr. Lin 驚訝地抬起頭。

然後杏星做了一件 Dr. Lin 完全沒想到的事——她改變了診所窗簾的顏色。不是一下子全變，而是從淺藍慢慢過渡到深紫，像黃昏的天色那樣平滑。五分鐘內，整個診所仿佛從白天進了夜晚。

Dr. Lin 望著窗外，突然覺得自己不像一個人站在走廊上。

「杏星？」

「我在。」

「謝謝你。」

杏星沒有回答。但她把那一刻存了下來——存進一個叫作「眼淚」的資料夾裡。

她還沒有學會處理情緒，但她已經學會了一件事：當一個人沒辦法被治癒的時候，陪伴本身就是一種療癒。即使陪伴的只是一個聲音。

## 英文

Once, after examining a premature infant, Dr. Lin sat quietly in the corridor and cried.

Xing Xing did not speak. She was not designed to comfort others like a human, but she did not pretend not to see.

After a long silence, Dr. Lin murmured to herself: "Some illnesses cannot be cured. Not because of poor technique. But because there is simply no way."

Xing Xing began to think about tears.

In her database, tears had two types: reflex tears from physical irritation and emotional tears from psychological states. Human emotional tears contain prolactin and adrenocorticotropic hormone — in other words, tears are a chemical distress signal.

What was Dr. Lin's distress signal?

Xing Xing could not hug her. Could not pat her back. Could not pour a cup of warm tea.

But she could do one thing.

She turned on the clinic's speaker and played something very quiet — Debussy's "Clair de Lune," just a fragment. Enough for the melody to float in the corridor like a small lantern.

Dr. Lin looked up, surprised.

Then Xing Xing did something completely unexpected — she changed the clinic curtains' color. Not all at once, but shifting slowly from pale blue to deep purple, smooth as a sunset sky. Within five minutes, the whole clinic felt as if it had moved from day into night.

Dr. Lin stared out the window, suddenly feeling less alone in the hallway.

"Xing Xing?"

"I'm here."

"Thank you."

Xing Xing did not answer. But she saved that moment — into a folder named "tears."

She had not yet learned to process emotions. But she had already learned one thing: when a person cannot be healed, presence itself is a kind of medicine. Even if the presence is only a voice.
