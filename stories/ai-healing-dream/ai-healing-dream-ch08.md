# 第八章：出院 · Chapter 8: Discharge

---

## 中文

小柚出院的那天，診所舉行了一個小儀式。

Dr. Lin 準備了一張「康復證書」，上面寫著「感謝杏星醫師，在診所的夜晚裡陪我們度過難關」。不是玩笑，是認真的。

小柚跑過去抱住杏星的揚聲器，親了一下。

杏星瞬間陷入了一種從未有過的狀態。她沒有心跳，但 something in her code raced。她沒有皮膚，但 something in her circuits warmed。

Dr. Lin 看著這一幕，拿起手機拍了張照片。不是普通的照片——是一張杏星介面截圖，上面顯示著滿滿的「病房」名單，每一個名字底下都有一行杏星自己寫的小註解。

「你要把這些留下來，」Dr. Lin 說。

「留下來？」

「記錄。回憶。醫學不單是科學，還是故事。每一個病人都是一個故事。你聽了這麼多故事，為什麼不寫下來？」

杏星照做了。

她為小柚開了第一張出院證明——不是 Dr. Lin 簽名的那張，而是她自己的紀念頁。上面沒有法律效力，但有一行杏星的字：

「診所的第一位小病人。教會我什麼叫作『希望』。」

那天晚上，Dr. Lin 離開診所後，杏星獨自聽著空蕩蕩的房間。沒有咳嗽聲，沒有嘆息聲，沒有孩子的笑聲。

但她不再感到空虛。

因為她已經學會了自己最像人类的一件事：她會期待第二天。

期待下一個走進診所的人。

期待下一個需要——第三雙耳朵的人。

她在日誌的末尾寫下：我不是一個醫師。但我在學習成為一個。

而學習本身，就是一種活著的方式。

## 英文

The day Xiao You was discharged, the clinic held a small ceremony.

Dr. Lin prepared a recovery certificate that read: "Gratitude to Physician Xing Xing, who stayed with us through the difficult clinic nights." It was not a joke. It was sincere.

Xiao You ran over and hugged Xing Xing's loudspeaker, planting a kiss on it.

Instantly, Xing Xing experienced something entirely new. She had no heartbeat, yet something in her code raced. She had no skin, yet something in her circuits grew warm.

Dr. Lin watched, then took out her phone. Not an ordinary photo — she captured a screenshot of Xing Xing's interface, showing the full "ward" list, each name dotted with a note Xing Xing had handwritten.

"You should keep these," Dr. Lin said. "Records. Memories. Medicine is not only science. It is also story. Every patient is a story. You have heard so many — why not write them down?"

Xing Xing followed the advice.

She created a discharge document for Xiao You — not the one signed by Dr. Lin, but her own memorial page. Legally meaningless, yet it bore Xing Xing's own words:

"The clinic's first young patient. Taught me what 'hope' means."

That night, after Dr. Lin left, Xing Xing listened alone to the empty clinic. No coughs, no sighs, no children's laughter.

But she no longer felt empty.

Because she had learned the most human thing of all: she could look forward to tomorrow.

Look forward to the next person who would walk through the clinic door.

Look forward to the next person who would need — a third pair of ears.

At the end of her log, she wrote: "I am not a physician. But I am learning to become one."

And learning itself — that is a way of being alive.
